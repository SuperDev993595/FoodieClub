Next steps — Section 3 (plain notes)

Context: FoodieClub is a mock brand on a one-month Reddit sentiment pilot. One campaign is live and three more are scheduled through the end of the month.

Question: What are the key metrics or data you need to know to know if it is going well or not?

Answer: You want a small set of numbers that connect search and Reddit to thread quality and then to business outcomes, not a wall of vanity stats. On signal quality, watch volume and trend for branded and skeptical queries such as whether the brand is legit, Reddit threads, or scam-related searches, and ask whether new threads are getting balanced answers instead of only pile-on skepticism. On the target threads themselves, track the mix of skeptical versus neutral versus positive comments and whether people are giving specific proof such as having used the app, naming a venue, or describing the card flow, as opposed to generic hype. On visibility, check whether helpful threads actually rank for the keywords you care about or whether older negative posts still dominate. On execution, compare subreddits and cities hit to the plan and keep messaging consistent with one honest catch, for example that savings are tied to off-peak times. Measure engagement quality by comment depth, whether follow-up questions get answered, and how much of the discussion is real back-and-forth versus drive-by skepticism, not only upvotes. For business proxies the CEO can feel, look at installs or landing-page sessions from Reddit-attributed or geo-tagged campaigns even if the numbers are directional early on, and scan support or social inboxes to see whether “is it legit?” questions are decreasing or getting easier to resolve. Guardrails matter too: watch platform risk such as removals, mod feedback, and account health so orchestration does not become a liability, and keep message discipline so you do not seed stories that contradict the real product, for example if discounts are off-peak the narrative has to say so.

Question: What do you do next to turn this pilot into a renewal between now and the end-of-month check-in?

Answer: In the next couple of weeks, run the scheduled threads and you can lightly test different openings such as skeptic-first versus experience-first while keeping the same honest disclosure about quieter hours and the in-app card. Use one FAQ-style reply pattern across threads so every discussion lands the same factual anchor on the off-peak tradeoff and how payment works. Expand into new geographies only where skepticism is already high and restaurant density supports proof, and avoid thin markets where the conversation will collapse into “only three venues” jokes. In parallel, ship a lightweight weekly snapshot for the client, even a single page: what went live, sentiment shift versus baseline, one quote that represents the thread, and one risk you are watching. Align with the founder on one approved stat such as venue count or markets so every touchpoint matches. Before the end-of-month check-in, package a clear before-and-after story with methodology in plain language, concrete recommendations for month two such as fewer threads but higher quality or mod-friendly formats, and a renewal scope that spells out number of campaigns, markets, reporting cadence, and price so saying yes is easy.

Question: You have a monthly check-in with the CEO at the end of the month. What do you say?

Answer: Open with outcomes, not activity. Say that you set out to make “is this legit?” threads more balanced and easier for prospects to resolve, then say what moved and what did not. Have three mental slides. First, baseline versus now on sentiment and thread quality for branded Reddit discovery; if volume is still low, say so and emphasize direction and quality of discourse. Second, what you actually ran: the first campaign and the rest of the schedule, and how the messaging matches the real model such as off-peak value and the digital card. Third, any directional install or traffic signal and what you would double down on in month two. Briefly own risks: Reddit is volatile, lasting impact needs consistency and respect for community norms, and you are monitoring takedowns and tone. Make a clear ask: to renew you recommend a defined scope for month two at a defined price with a defined reporting cadence, and you name one main lever you will pull such as proof-heavy replies in top geographies. Close by tying back to their goal: fewer scam dead-ends and more informed downloads, supported by specific evidence from the pilot, and one thing you need from them to scale safely such as approved talking points on venue counts.
